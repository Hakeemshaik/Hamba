Hamba Build & Launch Roadmap

Uber for moving & hauling · South Africa

The hard part isn't the app. It's supply and trust.

Your strategy is sound. This turns it into something executable for one Joburg corridor — and, just as importantly, into the order you should do it in. Built from a five-lens analysis plus an adversarial reality-check.

North star

Own ONE 15 km Joburg corridor end-to-end — 25–40 vetted bakkie owners, every paid booking contactable and fulfillable, dispatched by a human over WhatsApp — before building any algorithm, driver app, or live GPS.

Do this first

Prove demand before you build anything.

The single most important call in this whole document: do not build a backend or fund a standby driver fleet yet. Your own logic — "automate only what manual ops proves" — applies one level higher than the plan realises. Prove that paid demand exists before spending a rand on tech.

Run a 4-week, zero-code concierge test:

  • 1One WhatsApp number. You answer it.
  • 2Quote each job by hand; send a Yoco payment link manually.
  • 35 drivers paid per completed job only — no standby retainer.
  • 4A Google Sheet is your entire "system".

The kill-number: if you can't organically generate 15–20 paid, fulfilled moves a week in your corridor at a survivable cost by week 8 — stop. You'll have learned it for ≈R0 instead of R700k+. Everything below is downstream of this one test passing.

The three phases

Lean by design. Each phase is earned by the previous one working — not scheduled in advance.

Phase 0 — Make one real booking possible

Weeks 1–4

Turn the demo into something that can capture, confirm and fulfil a single real, contactable, paid booking — with honest pricing and zero false claims. (Most of this is the concierge test; the app build waits until demand is proven.)

  • Capture customer name + WhatsApp number before payment — today the app stores no customer identity at all, so a "booking" can't be contacted or fulfilled.
  • Pick ONE ~15 km corridor (lead bet: Sandton / Bryanston / Fourways / Midrand) and vet every driver in person.
  • Replace free-text addresses with a corridor suburb picker; quote a price range, not a fake-precise total.
  • Add T&Cs + a POPIA privacy notice + consent before payment; register an Information Officer (free, online).
  • Get a broker quote for goods-in-transit cover in writing — this is a go/no-go gate, not a later step.

Phase 1 — Real money, real fulfilment, real trust

Weeks 4–10

Take money safely, fulfil jobs via manual WhatsApp dispatch with engineered no-show resistance, and stand up the legal + insurance floor before the first paid house move.

  • Stand up a lean backend (Supabase: Postgres + phone-OTP + Realtime) and move pricing server-side. Never trust a browser-computed total.
  • Wire real Yoco via a server-created checkout + webhook. Use deposit-now / balance-on-completion, not full prepayment.
  • A /driver route in the same PWA (not a second app): see job, accept, press status buttons → drives an honest live status timeline.
  • Manual weekly EFT payouts with a transparent commission ledger. Yoco collects into your account; it does not pay drivers out.
  • Bind goods-in-transit + public-liability cover and attorney-reviewed T&Cs + contractor agreements before the first paid move.

Phase 2 — Automate only what manual ops proves

Month 3+ · earned

Once one corridor has steady liquidity and the human dispatcher is provably the bottleneck, automate the painful parts and clone the corridor.

  • Rules-based auto-dispatch; automated payouts via a licensed rail (Stitch / Ozow / Paystack).
  • Coarse live GPS pin while the driver app is open (background GPS is impossible in a PWA — don't promise it).
  • Ratings & driver scorecard; secondary revenue (insurance add-on, packing materials, storage referrals).
  • Clone the corridor playbook to a second belt — expansion is a data exercise, not a rebuild.

Where the plan was too optimistic

The adversarial pass flagged four assumptions that quietly decide whether this lives or dies. Corrected here so you don't learn them the expensive way.

The real cold-start burn is 2–4× bigger

Plan assumed: ≈R300k standby.

Closer to R700k–R1.2m once you add a dispatcher salary (~R15–25k/mo for 9 months), attorney work (R25–60k), vetting, the GIT premium, and a realistic CAC. A bakkie owner who can clear R1,500–2,500 on a freelance move won't sit on standby for R750. Set the hard kill-number and don't fund standby until demand is proven.

Moving is a near-zero-frequency purchase

Plan assumed: 20% repeat rate, <R150 organic CAC.

Households move every 3–7 years; rubble is one-off. There's no retention flywheel, so you pay near-full CAC forever (realistically R300–800, not R150). Fix: make B2B the primary channel — builders' merchants, estate/rental agents, self-storage, landscapers give schedulable, repeatable volume. Consumer house-moves are the topping, not the base.

Goods-in-transit insurance is a go/no-go gate

Plan assumed: "get a quote", ~R40–80/job.

SA underwriters often decline a rotating pool of casual owner-drivers with no telematics, or load it heavily. If blanket cover is unobtainable or uneconomic, your "Insured load" badge is undeliverable and the thin margin evaporates. Get it in writing before building — and if it fails, change the claim to "vetted & accountable," not "insured."

Disintermediation is structural, not a bug

Plan assumed: a no-circumvention clause protects you.

The job forces driver and customer to meet at a house and swap numbers. A good R2,200 move and they'll call the driver direct next time — a clause won't stop it. Design value around what they lose by going direct (insurance, guaranteed backup, dispute recourse) and lean on B2B accounts where the relationship is the contract, not the individual move.

You already have competition

Plan assumed: a clear field.

Wise Move already runs an SA moving-quote marketplace; Picup / Droppa do same-day delivery; thousands of Gumtree/OLX bakkies are cheaper and commission-free; established movers (Biddulphs, Stuttaford) own the high end. Your wedge is verified trust + reliability in one dense corridor — name it sharply against these, don't pretend they're absent.

Revenue, sequenced

Order matters — adding the wrong stream early kills the supply you're fighting to recruit.

Booking commission

RAMP 0→10→15→20%

Core revenue. Start at 0% to recruit; raise only once drivers visibly net more via Hamba than freelancing. Raising too early triggers defection back to cash WhatsApp jobs.

Insurance / GIT add-on

PHASE 1

Raises basket size and de-risks high-value moves — once the blanket policy is actually in force.

Packing materials

PHASE 2

Boxes, blankets, tape. Margin-positive, sourced via the same drivers/partners.

Storage referrals

PHASE 2

Driver subscriptions & featured listings

HOLD

Only once drivers compete for jobs. Charging scarce early drivers to be on the platform will kill recruitment.

Top risks

Launch blocker

False trust & insurance claims

The UI shows "ID verified", "Insured load", "Vetted drivers" with no backend and no real vetting. Under the Consumer Protection Act these are enforceable representations — going live like this is a live misrepresentation and direct liability the moment goods are damaged. Gate every badge behind a real per-driver flag.

Launch blocker

The Yoco payout gap

Yoco collects the full fare into your account — it has no native split/escrow/payout to independent drivers in SA. Paying drivers is a separate, initially manual process; spending money you owe them is a trust-killer and a possible unlicensed-payment issue. Run an honest manual EFT ledger first.

High

Two-sided cold-start sequencing

Tech can be ready in weeks, but with no verified drivers there are no jobs to dispatch. Vetting + insurance + manual ops must be live before demand marketing — or paid bookings get refunded with no driver, damaging the exact reliability you're selling.

High

Driver misclassification (LRA s200A)

Branding "your driver", assigning jobs and fixing prices pushes toward deemed employment — retroactive PAYE / UIF / leave exposure across the whole base. Keep drivers free to accept/decline and work elsewhere; get the contractor agreement attorney-reviewed.

High

POPIA & customer safety

You collect addresses, empty-home dates and (literally) "gate codes" with no consent notice. A moving app uniquely tells a bad actor when a home is empty. Capture consent now, minimise data, mask drop-off until acceptance, and proxy phone numbers later.
